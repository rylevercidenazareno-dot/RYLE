What is Arduino?

Arduino is an open-source electronics platform based on easy-to-use hardware and software. It is designed to help students, hobbyists, engineers, and developers create interactive electronic projects by combining programmable microcontrollers with sensors, actuators, and communication modules.

Arduino boards are programmed using the Arduino IDE with C/C++ programming language. They can read inputs from sensors, switches, and buttons, then process the information to control outputs such as LEDs, motors, relays, LCD displays, buzzers, and other electronic devices.

Arduino has become one of the most popular platforms for learning embedded systems because of its simplicity, flexibility, and large community support. It is widely used in robotics, Internet of Things (IoT), smart home automation, industrial monitoring, environmental sensing, and educational projects.

Applications of Arduino

🏠 Smart Home Automation

Controls lighting, appliances, doors, and environmental monitoring systems.

🤖 Robotics

Controls robot movement, sensors, obstacle detection, and automation.

🌡 Environmental Monitoring

Collects temperature, humidity, gas, and weather data using sensors.

🌐 Internet of Things (IoT)

Connects devices to the internet for remote monitoring and automation.

🏭 Industrial Automation

Monitors equipment and controls industrial processes.

🎓 Education & Prototyping

Used by students and engineers for learning electronics and rapid prototype development.
