What is Embedded Systems?

An embedded system is a specialized computer system designed to perform a specific function within a larger device. Unlike general-purpose computers, embedded systems are built to execute dedicated tasks efficiently, reliably, and in real time.

Embedded systems integrate hardware and software to control electronic devices. They commonly use microcontrollers such as Arduino, ESP32, PIC, or STM32, together with sensors, actuators, communication modules, and displays.

These systems are widely applied in smart homes, industrial automation, robotics, automotive electronics, medical equipment, consumer electronics, and Internet of Things (IoT) devices.
